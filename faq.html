Frequently Asked Questions

Cellular

Coverage systems must be designed to provide optimal indoor signal without creating interference ("noise") on licensed cellular networks.

Since carriers (like Bell, Telus, Rogers, etc.) pay billions for their RF spectrum rights, any rebroadcast of their signal requires both their explicit permission and a technically-vetted design. Rogue cellular boosters violate these crucial rules and are subject to punishment by both the license holder and the government.

Wi-Fi

To ensure your investment delivers the required coverage, capacity, and security, a Professional Wi-Fi Site Survey is essential.

Performing a survey before deployment helps minimize costs by determining the optimal number and location of access points. We assist clients in identifying the necessary survey type (passive or active) to meet their specific requirements.

Furthermore, a proper survey and channel planning are necessary to:

  • Identify and resolve channel conflicts and interference.
  • Detect rogue access points set up by hackers.
  • Limit the network's physical reach to prevent external security breaches.

Wi-Fi performance issues stem from multiple factors, including poor coverage, insufficient capacity, and interference.

Productive Wi-Fi site surveys are necessary to uncover areas where coverage, speed, security, and settings can be improved. Since the wireless field rapidly advances, regular surveys ensure continuous enhancement of your network quality, which directly impacts your company's productivity.

Designing a reliable wireless network requires professional tools and years of experience to avoid wasting time and money.

Our design process begins with a detailed customer questionnaire to understand your specific coverage and capacity needs. We then apply our expertise to manipulate complex RF design parameters (including SNIR, RSSI, interference, and coverage overlap) to ensure your network investment directly contributes to your team's productivity and success.

Wi-Fi 7 is the latest generation wireless networking protocol, officially known as IEEE 802.11be.

Wi-Fi 7 offers several improvements over previous generations. Linkwave's professionals will help you understand in a clear and concise manner the importance of upgrading your network.
