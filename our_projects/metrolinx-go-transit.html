Case Study • Transit Wi‑Fi

Metrolinx Wi‑Fi installation

With Linkwave’s support, Metrolinx launched the first GO Transit buses and trains equipped with Icomera’s centralized connectivity platform—delivering reliable onboard Wi‑Fi across the GTA. Linkwave partnered closely with Icomera Canada to manage implementation and commissioning for the fleet.

GO buses & trains Icomera platform Greater Toronto Area Implementation & commissioning
Overview Outcomes Challenges Design Project Management Commissioning

Project Overview

Metrolinx selected Icomera Canada to deploy a powerful onboard connectivity solution across GO Transit’s fleet. As a proud partner, Linkwave managed the implementation and commissioning phases and maintained an up‑to‑date installation and commissioning database. Working through pandemic constraints, the team enhanced customer‑provided software tools to automate workflows, improve efficiency, and reduce errors—keeping the program on time and on budget.

Key Highlights

  • First GO Transit vehicles equipped with a centralized onboard Wi‑Fi platform
  • End‑to‑end implementation management and commissioning led by Linkwave
  • Continuously updated installation/commissioning database for all stakeholders
  • Enhanced commissioning tools for greater automation and fewer errors

Deployment Realities on Rolling Stock

Limited Space & Harsh Conditions

Mechanical, electrical, and RF plans had to fit tight spaces and endure vibration, temperature swings, and exposure common to buses and rail cars.

Safety & Technical Regulations

Transit authority requirements shaped equipment placement, cable routing, and approvals, narrowing deployment options and demanding precision.

Equipment Variants

Fleet diversity introduced mounting and integration differences, requiring careful documentation and attention to detail across vehicles.

Execution at Scale

A constantly updated install/commissioning database aligned Icomera, Metrolinx, Bombardier, and Linkwave on progress, issues, and resolutions.

Design

Designing Wi‑Fi for moving vehicles differs from typical in‑building systems. The team balanced mechanical, electrical, and RF constraints, adhering to transit safety standards while accommodating hardware variations across a large fleet. Linkwave collaborated with Icomera, Bombardier, and Metrolinx engineers to finalize a robust, compliant design—delivered on schedule and within budget.

Project Management

Linkwave led implementation and commissioning across buses and trains, providing project management support to Icomera, Metrolinx, and Bombardier. Enhanced commissioning tools improved automation and reduced errors, while rigorous tracking ensured stakeholders had current status at every stage.

Testing, Commissioning and Optimization

Onboard Wi‑Fi depends on both cellular backhaul and the Wi‑Fi network itself. The solution underwent thorough testing against strict transit engineering guidelines, with commissioning across rail and bus fleets achieving 100% performance acceptance and reliable day‑to‑day operation.
