Ontario Power Generation (Darlington)

To overcome challenging macro coverage at the Darlington Energy Complex, OPG selected Linkwave through a public RFP to design, implement, and commission a multi‑carrier DAS—now expanding to connect additional buildings across the Darlington Nuclear site.

Multi‑carrier DAS CommScope IonB Phase II expansion Public RFP award
Overview Design Project Management Commissioning

Project Overview

The Darlington Energy Complex (DEC) is central to the Darlington Refurbishment and ongoing operations of the nuclear station. With the facility located more than 2 km from all WSP towers, macro coverage was insufficient indoors. Through a public RFP, OPG selected Linkwave to design, implement, and commission a distributed antenna system that delivers carrier‑grade service across the DEC—and scales to additional buildings in subsequent phases.

Design

A multi‑carrier CommScope IonB platform was selected for its effectiveness and simplicity in delivering all carriers, all bands, and all technologies. The architecture was engineered for Phase II expansion to connect new buildings at Darlington Nuclear to a single, centralized DAS—ensuring an efficient, cost‑effective, and maintainable solution.

Project Management / Construction Support

In partnership with Black & McDonald, Linkwave supervised the installation phase, established test procedures, and set construction acceptance criteria. We coordinated with all wireless operators to ensure agreements were finalized before the commissioning date, maintaining alignment and schedule integrity.

Commissioning and Optimization

Commissioning focused on protecting OPG’s investment through a “healthy” DAS operation. Our team conducted exhaustive walk tests to verify every antenna, enforced alignment with pre‑commissioning calculations, and iterated until DEC stakeholders were fully satisfied with performance.
