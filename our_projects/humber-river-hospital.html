Humber River Hospital

Ontario’s first digital hospital: a carrier‑approved, public‑safety‑ready DAS with 300+ radiating points engineered for a 17‑story, 1.7–1.8M sq ft facility.

17 stories 1.7–1.8M sq ft 300+ points Public safety + 900 MHz
Overview Outcomes Challenges Design Project Management Commissioning

Project Overview

Humber River Hospital is one of Canada’s largest regional acute care hospitals — a 17‑story, approximately 1.7–1.8 million sq. ft. facility with 656 beds, 3,300 staff, about 700 physicians and 400 volunteers. The hospital is a technological and environmental showcase, and the DAS designed by Linkwave is part of it.

Key Outcomes

  • Carrier‑approved multi‑carrier DAS covering the entire 17‑story facility
  • Public safety and private 900 MHz integrated alongside cellular bands
  • 300+ radiating points engineered on TE Spectrum platform
  • On‑schedule readiness despite construction and carrier timeline constraints

Key Challenges & How We Solved Them

Construction‑Phase Deployment

Coordinated closely with GC and trades; staged deliveries and protected risers and trays to prevent damage.

Carrier Schedule Variability

Implemented temporary integration plan to bridge delayed operator equipment and maintain the project schedule.

Public Safety Requirements

Extra QA and bylaw compliance validation; worked with AHJ to certify coverage and system resiliency.

Large‑Scale Commissioning

Detailed test plans, floor‑by‑floor optimization, and continuous feedback loops with hospital teams and carriers.

Design

An ambitious multi‑carrier design accommodating licensed cellular bands, public safety frequencies, and private 900 MHz radio. Built on the TE Spectrum platform with 300+ radiating points, approved by all national carriers, public safety authorities, and the private radio licensee.

Project Management

Installed during general construction with dynamic schedules and construction hazards. We protected DAS infrastructure, mitigated risks, and maintained timeline alignment with carriers. Public safety segments received heightened attention. All Neutral Host operators were connected successfully and on time.

Testing, Commissioning and Optimization

Commissioning a system of this size required careful planning, preparation and patient execution. Our engineers and technicians worked hand‑in‑hand with hospital users and carriers to validate KPIs and ensure stakeholder satisfaction.
